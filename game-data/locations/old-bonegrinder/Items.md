# Items in Old Bonegrinder

## Available Items (If Hags Are Defeated)

### Ground Floor - Millstone Room
- **Sacks of "Flour":** Multiple sacks containing the ground bone/flour mixture. Evidence of the operation. Can be destroyed or taken as proof of the hags' crimes.
- **Grinding Mechanism Parts:** Iron gears, chains, and millstone components. Salvageable for scrap metal (50 gp worth total) or to disable the mill permanently.
- **Basic Tools:** Hammers, chisels, brooms, cleaning supplies (2 gp value)

### Second Floor - Kitchen and Living Quarters
- **Dream Pastries (12 remaining):** The infamous pastries. Each provides 1 hour of euphoric hallucinations (Constitution DC 16 save or become addicted, requiring more pastries or suffer withdrawal). Can be sold for 1 gp each to addicts or destroyed to prevent spread.
- **Baking Equipment:** Mixing bowls, rolling pins, baking sheets, brick oven (25 gp value if dismantled)
- **Spell Components:** Jar collection containing various components worth approximately 100 gp total
  - Eye of newt (standard component)
  - Powdered silver (10 gp)
  - Dried herbs (various, 5 gp)
  - Pickled organs (disturbing but valuable for necromancy, 15 gp)
  - Bottled tears (emotion component, 20 gp)
  - Ground bone meal (from victims, 0 gp moral value)
- **Hag's Recipe Book:** Leather-bound book containing pastry recipe (including horrible truth about ingredients), potion recipes, and notes on various victims. Evidence and source of dark culinary knowledge.
- **Coinage:** Hidden in various locations throughout kitchen
  - 156 gp in various pouches
  - 89 sp scattered in jars
  - 200 cp in a sock
- **Jewelry (Children's):** Lockets, rings, and trinkets taken from victims. 12 pieces worth 5-25 gp each (total ~180 gp). Returning to families would be heroic but difficult.

### Second Floor - Locked Cabinet (DC 15 Thieves' Tools or DC 18 Strength)
- **Heartstone (Morgantha's):** Black gemstone pulsing with dark energy. The source of a night hag's power, allows Ethereal travel. Worth 500 gp to dark magic practitioners, but possibly more valuable for preventing Morgantha's return if she escaped.
- **Soul Bag:** Small black bag containing 6 soul larvae (corrupted souls of evil mortals). Hags trade these in the Lower Planes. Worth 200 gp each on black market, but possessing them is evil act.
- **Potion of Healing (Greater):** 4d4+4 HP restoration, 200 gp value
- **Potion of Poison:** Can be used to poison food/drink, 100 gp
- **Scroll of Protection from Good and Evil:** Single-use spell scroll, 100 gp

### Third Floor - Children's Prison
- **Crude Cages:** Poorly built but functional. Contain nothing of value except the imprisoned children.
- **Children's Belongings:** Freek's wooden toy horse, Myrtle's rag doll, clothes, a stolen copper piece. No monetary value, but significant emotional value to the children.
- **Previous Victims' Effects:** Scattered in corners, mostly destroyed. A few intact items:
  - Child's silver necklace (10 gp)
  - Small prayer book to the Morninglord (meaningful but no monetary value)
  - Torn pieces of clothing (evidence of victims)

### Roof and Mechanism
- **Windmill Components:** Massive wooden blades, axle, gears. Could be salvaged for lumber and iron (200 gp worth if willing to invest time and labor disassembling).
- **Wereraven Feather:** Single black feather left intentionally. Shows that Keepers of the Feather are watching.

### Basement Storage
- **Flour Sacks (Actual):** 10 sacks of legitimate flour mixed with ground bones. Each contaminated, worth nothing.
- **Barrel of Water:** Fresh water, useful for survival but not valuable.
- **Preserved Foods:** Salted meats, dried vegetables. Weeks of rations for 3 people, worth ~15 gp.
- **Bone Collection (Victim Remains):** Horrifying evidence. Multiple barrels and sacks containing sorted bones from dozens of children over many years.
  - Skulls: 37 total (representing 37 confirmed victims)
  - Long bones: sorted for grinding
  - Small bones: collected in sacks
  - No monetary value, but critical evidence of the hags' crimes
- **Deed to Old Bonegrinder:** Legal document signed by Gustav Durst (Death House), selling the windmill to Morgantha 15 years ago for "services rendered." Connects to Death House location and reveals Durst family's evil dealings.
- **Letters and Correspondence:** Several letters between Morgantha and various customers, including:
  - Letter from anonymous Vallakian noble requesting "discrete delivery"
  - Letter from Village of Barovia customer begging for more pastries, offering their savings
  - Disturbing letter offering children in trade for pastries (unsigned)
- **Iron Lockbox (DC 18 Thieves' Tools or DC 20 Strength):**
  - 340 gp (life savings from various transactions)
  - Jeweled ring worth 150 gp (probably taken from victim)
  - Small black pearl worth 100 gp (spell component)
  - Contract written in Infernal: agreement between Morgantha and a devil regarding soul trading (reveals hags' extraplanar connections)

## Hidden Items (Requires Investigation)

### Ground Floor
- **Hidden Compartment in Wall:** DC 15 Investigation near grinding stones. Contains emergency supplies:
  - 2 Potions of Healing (standard, 2d4+2)
  - Silvered dagger (50 gp, for defending against lycanthropes)
  - 75 gp in emergency coin
  - Scroll of Dimension Door (escape spell, 500 gp value)

### Second Floor
- **Under Floorboard:** DC 16 Investigation in Morgantha's sleeping alcove. Contains her personal diary written in coded Elvish (DC 14 Intelligence to decode). Reveals:
  - Names and locations of regular customers throughout Barovia
  - Notes about "the master" (Strahd) and his tolerance of her operation
  - Mentions of other hags in Barovia and potential coven allies
  - References to "the things in the gullet" (hints about Amber Temple)

### Basement
- **False Bottom Barrel:** DC 18 Investigation. Contains Morgantha's most valuable possessions:
  - Spellbook (contains Identify, Ray of Enfeeblement, Crown of Madness, Sleep, Hold Person)
  - Hag Eye (allows coven to see through it remotely, dangerous to possess)
  - 3 gems worth 100 gp each
  - Map of Barovia marking other dark power locations
  - Ancient letter from "the Dark Powers" acknowledging the coven's pact

## Quest Items

### Dream Pastries (Multiple Locations)
- **Critical Role:** These pastries are the reason for the entire operation. They're addictive and provide temporary escape from Barovia's horror. Party must decide what to do with remaining stockâ€”destroy them (preventing further addiction but enraging addicts), distribute them freely (causing addiction), or secure them for evidence.

### Children's Belonging Evidence
- **Freek's Identification:** Toy horse that can be returned to his mother in Village of Barovia (though she may be too addicted to care)
- **Myrtle's Rag Doll:** Only possession linking her to her past (parents deceased, no home to return to)
- **Previous Victims:** Items that could identify other victims and allow their families to know their fate

### Windmill Deed
- **Significance:** Connects Old Bonegrinder to Death House and the Durst family's evil history. Proof that Gustav Durst sold property to night hags "for services rendered," suggesting the Dursts' cult may have worked with the coven. This document provides lore connections and possible leads to other locations.

### Morgantha's Map
- **Strategic Value:** If found, reveals locations of other evil sites in Barovia: Amber Temple (vaguely marked), Berez (marked as "sister's domain" - Baba Lysaga), and various haunted locations. Useful for campaign planning but also attracts danger if Strahd or other dark powers learn party has it.

## Notes

### Loot Ethics
- Much of the treasure here comes from murdered children and desperate addicts
- Party must decide whether to keep victim belongings or return them to families
- Dream pastries represent moral dilemma: destroy them vs. use them as trade goods
- Some items (soul bag, Infernal contracts) are inherently evil to possess

### Hag Heartstones
- If any hag escapes, her Heartstone must be found to prevent her return
- Heartstones allow night hags to travel Ethereally and haunt victims
- Destroying all three Heartstones permanently ends the coven threat
- If party only defeats one or two hags, survivors will eventually reform coven

### Connection to Broader Campaign
- Deed connects to Death House (Story 4-5 Task 1)
- Wereraven presence connects to Wizard of Wines (Story 4-5 Task 4)
- Customer lists reveal NPCs in Vallaki and Village of Barovia who are addicted
- Strahd's tolerance of the operation reveals his cruel pragmatism
