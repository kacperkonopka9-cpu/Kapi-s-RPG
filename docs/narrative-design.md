# Kapi-s-RPG - Narrative Design Document

**Author:** Kapi
**Game Type:** Text-Based RPG / Interactive Fiction (LLM-Powered D&D 5e Platform)
**Narrative Complexity:** Critical (Story IS the game)

---

## Executive Summary

### Narrative Premise

Pulled into the cursed land of Barovia by mysterious mists, the player becomes trapped in a gothic horror nightmare ruled by the vampire lord Strahd von Zarovich. To escape this Dread Domain and save the innocent soul of Ireena Kolyana (reincarnation of Strahd's lost love Tatyana), the player must gather legendary artifacts, forge alliances with desperate survivors, and ultimately confront an immortal evil who has manipulated this land for centuries. But Barovia is more than a prison—it's a test of hope against despair, where every choice matters and the line between hero and monster grows ever thinner.

### Core Themes

1. **Hope vs. Despair** - Can light survive in a world designed to extinguish it? Barovia tests whether hope can persist in the face of overwhelming darkness.

2. **Obsession and Corruption** - Strahd's obsessive love for Tatyana/Ireena has corrupted him utterly. The player witnesses how obsession destroys both the obsessed and those around them.

3. **Free Will vs. Fate** - Are the players and NPCs truly free, or are they pawns in Strahd's eternal game? Can mortals defy a predetermined cycle?

4. **Redemption and Sacrifice** - Many Barovians are broken by their circumstances. Can anyone find redemption? What must be sacrificed to save others?

5. **The Price of Power** - The Amber Temple and dark gifts tempt players with power at terrible costs. How much of yourself will you trade for strength?

### Tone and Atmosphere

**Gothic Horror with Melancholic Beauty**

The narrative tone is oppressive yet hauntingly beautiful—Barovia is a land of vibrant autumnal colors drained of joy, where every sunset might be your last. The atmosphere balances genuine horror (body horror, psychological dread, existential terror) with tragic romanticism (doomed love, noble sacrifice, corrupted beauty).

**Emotional Palette:**
- Melancholy and isolation
- Creeping dread and paranoia
- Brief moments of warmth that make the darkness more poignant
- Tragic inevitability tinged with defiant hope
- Gothic grandeur amid decay

**Writing Style:**
- Rich, sensory prose (150-300 words per location)
- Evocative rather than explicit horror
- Dark poetry in descriptions
- NPCs speak with distinct voices reflecting their trauma and circumstances
- Silence and absence used as narrative tools

## Story Structure

### Story Type

**Modified 3-Act Structure with Open-World Exploration**

The campaign follows a loose 3-act framework but allows significant player agency in pacing and order of events. Story beats can be discovered non-linearly, with the Tarokka card reading providing a personalized quest structure for each playthrough.

### Act Breakdown

**Act 1: Arrival and Entrapment (Levels 1-3)**

*The Hook and the Horror*

- **Opening:** Player enters Barovia through the mists (involuntarily). The gates close behind them—escape is impossible.
- **Inciting Incident:** Arrival at the Village of Barovia. Meet Ismark "the Lesser" and discover Ireena Kolyana, hunted by Strahd.
- **Call to Adventure:** Ireena needs protection. The Burgomaster is dead. Strahd's letter welcomes the player to dinner at Castle Ravenloft (ominous invitation).
- **Crossing the Threshold:** Death House (optional but recommended)—a haunted mansion that teaches players Barovia's true nature through horror.
- **Act 1 Climax:** Tarokka reading with Madam Eva reveals the location of three legendary artifacts and where the final confrontation with Strahd will occur. The quest is defined.

**Key Emotional Beats:**
- Confusion → Dread → Determination
- Establishing Barovia as a prison with no easy escape
- First encounters with Strahd establish him as charming, terrifying, and seemingly omnipotent

**Act 2: The Long Dark (Levels 4-8)**

*Exploration, Allies, and Escalation*

- **Rising Action:** Open-world exploration phase. Player travels Barovia gathering artifacts, allies, and information.
- **Midpoint Reversal:** Major confrontation or revelation about Strahd's true nature, Tatyana's curse, or Barovia's history. Realization that defeating Strahd requires more than strength—it requires understanding him.
- **Complications:** Strahd actively interferes. NPCs die or are corrupted. Factions vie for player's loyalty. The Amber Temple tempts with dark powers.
- **Act 2 Climax:** All three artifacts acquired. Allies assembled (or lost). Player as ready as they'll ever be to assault Castle Ravenloft.

**Key Emotional Beats:**
- Hope through discovery of allies and artifacts
- Despair through NPC deaths and Strahd's power
- Moral complexity through difficult choices (dark gifts, faction conflicts)
- Building tension as Strahd's attention focuses on the player

**Major Sub-Arcs:**
- **Ireena's Fate:** Protect her from Strahd while uncovering her connection to Tatyana
- **Van Richten's Hunt:** The vampire hunter's vendetta and tragic backstory
- **Vallaki's Collapse:** Political intrigue and the illusion of safety
- **The Winery Crisis:** Druids vs. vintners—Barovia's last source of hope (wine)
- **The Abbot's Madness:** Twisted redemption in Krezk
- **The Amber Temple:** Ancient evil and temptation

**Act 3: The Assault (Levels 9-10+)**

*Confrontation and Resolution*

- **The Approach:** Journey to Castle Ravenloft. Last preparations. Final conversations with allies.
- **The Infiltration:** Navigate the massive castle dungeon (60+ rooms). Avoid or fight Strahd's minions.
- **The Climax:** Final confrontation with Strahd von Zarovich in the location determined by Tarokka reading.
- **Resolution:** Multiple possible endings based on choices and outcomes.

**Key Emotional Beats:**
- Determination despite fear
- Catharsis through final confrontation
- Loss (likely some allies die)
- Variable resolution (triumph, tragedy, or bittersweet victory)

### Story Beats

**Major Narrative Moments (Flexible Order):**

1. **Mists Claim the Player** - Involuntary entrance to Barovia
2. **Welcome to Barovia** - First glimpse of the cursed land; oppressive atmosphere
3. **Death House Horror** - Optional tutorial dungeon showing Barovia's true nature
4. **Meet Ireena** - The innocent caught in Strahd's obsession
5. **Strahd's Invitation** - The vampire lord's mocking welcome letter
6. **Funeral for the Burgomaster** - Burying Kolyan Indirovich; first priestly rites fail
7. **Tarokka Reading** - Madam Eva's fortunetelling sets the quest
8. **First Encounter with Strahd** - Meeting the villain (often on the road or at dinner)
9. **Vallaki's False Festivals** - The Baron's desperate attempt to enforce happiness
10. **The Wizard of Wines** - Druids corrupt Barovia's last hope (wine production)
11. **Argynvostholt** - Ruins of the Silver Dragon order; tragic backstory
12. **The Abbot's Creation** - The mad angel trying to create a bride for Strahd
13. **Amber Temple Temptation** - Dark gifts offer power at terrible cost
14. **Strahd Claims a Victim** - Major NPC death or corruption (Ireena, ally, or innocent)
15. **Van Richten Revealed** - The legendary vampire hunter's identity exposed
16. **Krezk Pool Miracle** - Potential redemption for Ireena (or tragedy)
17. **The Third Artifact** - Finally acquiring all legendary items
18. **Approach to Ravenloft** - The castle looms; no turning back
19. **Heart of Darkness** - Discovering Strahd's crypt and true nature
20. **Final Confrontation** - Epic battle with Count Strahd von Zarovich
21. **Escape or Entrapment** - Resolution and potential freedom from Barovia

### Pacing and Flow

**Pacing Strategy:**

- **Act 1 (Fast):** 3-5 sessions establishing horror and stakes
- **Act 2 (Slow to Medium):** 20-35 sessions of exploration with flexible pacing
- **Act 3 (Fast):** 5-8 sessions of climactic confrontation

**Tension Curve:**

The narrative uses a "rising tension with release valves" structure:
- **High-Tension Locations:** Death House, Amber Temple, Old Bonegrinder, Castle Ravenloft
- **Medium-Tension Hubs:** Villages (Vallaki, Krezk) provide respite but underlying dread
- **Low-Tension Moments:** Campfire conversations, Vistani camps, brief victories

**Story-Heavy vs. Gameplay-Heavy:**

- **Story-Dense:** Death House, Tarokka reading, dinner with Strahd, major NPC encounters
- **Exploration-Dense:** Wilderness travel, dungeon delving, artifact hunting
- **Balanced:** Village social intrigue, faction quests, mystery investigation

**Player Control:**

Players control pacing through:
- Choice of which locations to visit and when
- Engagement with optional content (extensive)
- Pursuit of side quests vs. main story
- Interactions with NPCs (can speed through or deeply roleplay)

## Characters

### Protagonist(s)

**The Player Character (Customizable)**

Since this is a D&D campaign, the protagonist is player-created with significant flexibility.

**Archetypal Role:** The Outsider / The Chosen

**Framework:**
- **Entrance:** Pulled into Barovia by the mists (unwilling participant)
- **Motivation:** Initially survival and escape; evolves into protecting Ireena and defeating Strahd
- **Internal Conflict:** Maintaining hope and humanity in a land designed to crush both
- **External Conflict:** Strahd and the hostile environment of Barovia

**Character Arc (Player-Driven):**
- **Start:** Confused outsider seeking escape
- **Middle:** Reluctant hero gathering power and allies
- **End (Variable):**
  - Triumphant liberator who defeats Strahd
  - Tragic victim consumed by Barovia
  - Corrupted replacement who becomes the new Strahd
  - Scarred survivor who escapes but forever changed

**Narrative Support:**
- Player's background informs how NPCs react
- Class and abilities affect available solutions
- Moral choices shape player's relationship with darkness
- Journal entries reflect player's psychological state

### Antagonist(s)

**Count Strahd von Zarovich - The Vampire Lord**

**Description:** Once a warrior-prince who conquered the valley, Strahd made a pact with dark powers to achieve immortality and claim Tatyana, a woman he loved but who did not love him. She died fleeing from him, and he became a vampire. For centuries, he has ruled Barovia as both lord and prisoner, watching Tatyana reincarnate again and again, always losing her.

**Motivation:**
- **Obsessive Love:** Possess Tatyana/Ireena; break the cycle of loss
- **Pride:** Prove his dominance over all who enter his domain
- **Despair:** He hates his existence but cannot escape it; destroying him may be mercy

**Methods:**
- **Manipulation:** Strahd is charming, intelligent, and plays long games
- **Surveillance:** Spies (wolves, bats, Vistani) watch everything
- **Psychological Warfare:** Appears and disappears, toys with prey
- **Direct Violence:** When diplomacy fails, he is a CR 15 terror

**Relationship to Protagonist:**
- Views player as entertainment, threat, or potential successor
- May offer dark bargains or show respect for worthy opponents
- Could see player as obstacle to claiming Ireena

**Sympathetic Elements:**
- Tragic origin (loved and lost Tatyana)
- Prisoner of his own curse
- Moments of melancholy and self-awareness
- Genuine love for his people (twisted as it is)

**Complex Villain Portrayal:**

Strahd is not pure evil—he's a tragic figure corrupted by obsession. The narrative presents him as:
- Charming and cultured (patron of arts, gracious host)
- Terrifying and ruthless (kills without hesitation)
- Melancholic and self-aware (knows he's a monster)
- Obsessed and delusional (believes Ireena will love him)

**Key Strahd Moments:**
- Invitation to dinner (shows hospitality and menace)
- Witnessing him play organ in Ravenloft (melancholic artist)
- Claiming a victim (brutal reminder of his nature)
- Final confrontation (desperate and magnificent)

**Secondary Antagonists:**

**Baba Lysaga - The Swamp Witch**
- Strahd's obsessed "mother figure"
- Believes she birthed/raised him
- Defends Berez ruins with fanatical devotion

**Rahadin - Strahd's Chamberlain**
- Dusk elf who betrayed his people for Strahd
- Utterly loyal; followed Strahd into damnation
- Screaming souls of his victims surround him

**The Dark Powers**
- Mysterious entities that created Barovia as a prison
- No physical form; work through the land itself
- Tempt through the Amber Temple

### Supporting Characters

**Ireena Kolyana - The Reincarnated Love**

**Role:** Damsel, but with agency and complexity

**Personality:** Brave, compassionate, haunted by dreams she doesn't understand. Refuses to be passive victim. Feels guilty that others die protecting her.

**Relationship to Protagonist:** Ally, potential companion, quest objective

**Function in Story:** MacGuffin with humanity; her fate is central to defeating Strahd

**Character Arc:** From frightened target → active participant in own destiny → variable ending (salvation, sacrifice, or tragedy)

**Key Scenes:**
- First meeting in Barovia village
- Revealing her Tatyana connection
- Potential redemption at Krezk pool
- Final confrontation decisions

**Role:** Fallen angel, tragic villain

  - Realizing she is Tatyana reborn
  - Choosing to fight rather than hide
  - Potential self-sacrifice to save others

  - **Redemption:** United with Sergei in Krezk pool; escapes reincarnation cycle

  - **Escaped:** Leaves Barovia with player (freedom)

**Rudolph van Richten's Arc:**

- **Starting State:** Disguised vampire hunter consumed by vengeance; trusts no one
- **Transformation Moments:**
  - Revealing identity to player
  - Confronting whether revenge will bring peace
  - Choosing between survival and sacrifice
- **Ending State:**
  - **Redemption:** Sacrifices self to save others; finds peace
  - **Survival:** Defeats Strahd and retires from hunting
  - **Corruption:** Becomes as ruthless as monsters he hunts

- **Starting State:** Outsider with no stake in Barovia
- **Transformation Moments:**
  - First NPC death they could have prevented
  - Tarokka reading making them "chosen"
  - Temptation at Amber Temple
  - Realizing escape requires defeating Strahd
- **Ending State:**
  - **Hero:** Defeats Strahd and escapes as better person
  - **Survivor:** Escapes but scarred and changed
  - **Corrupted:** Defeats Strahd but becomes new Dark Lord

- **Starting State:** Broken by guilt over sister's death
- **Transformation:** Seeks Amber Temple to resurrect her
- **Ending State:**
  - **Corruption:** Accepts dark gift; becomes villain
  - **Acceptance:** Lets go of past; finds peace
  - **Success:** Resurrects sister (but at what cost?)

**The Abbot's Arc:**

- **Starting State:** Insane angel believing he can fix Barovia
- **Transformation:** Player can kill, redeem, or enable him
- **Ending State:**
  - **Death:** Put out of his misery
  - **Redemption:** Realizes his error; helps fight Strahd
  - **Tragedy:** Completes his creation; fails anyway

## World and Lore

### World Overview

**Setting:** Barovia - A Dread Domain in the Shadowfell

**Time Period:** Medieval Eastern European (roughly 15th-16th century equivalent)

**World Type:** Demiplane prison created by mysterious Dark Powers

**Geography:**
- Valley surrounded by impassable mountains and perpetual fog
- Approximately 20-30 miles across
- Contains villages, forests, mountains, ruins, and Castle Ravenloft

**World Rules:**

**The Mists:**
- Surround Barovia completely
- Prevent escape (anyone entering vanishes and reappears in Barovia)
- Occasionally pull new victims into the domain
- May be sentient or guided by Dark Powers

**Eternal Stagnation:**
- Sun never truly shines (overcast daylight at best)
- Nothing truly grows or improves
- Hope is constantly tested and often crushed
- Magic works differently (resurrection restricted, etc.)

**Strahd's Dominion:**
- Strahd can sense major events in his realm
- Animals spy for him (wolves, bats, ravens)
- He cannot be permanently killed while in Barovia (reforms in coffin)
- Dark Powers prevent his escape

**Magic and Technology:**
- D&D 5e magic functions (with resurrection restrictions)
- Medieval technology level
- No gunpowder (except Van Richten's limited supply)
- Superstition and folklore hold truth

**Atmosphere and Aesthetics:**

**Visual Identity:**
- Gothic horror meets Eastern European folklore
- Vibrant autumn colors (reds, golds, oranges) made oppressive
- Perpetual overcast skies; rare sunbreaks feel miraculous
- Architecture: stone, wood, crumbling grandeur
- Nature reclaiming civilization

**Sensory Palette:**
- **Sight:** Fog, shadows, candlelight, blood reds, sickly greens
- **Sound:** Distant wolves, creaking wood, thunder, silence
- **Smell:** Decay, damp earth, smoke, blood
- **Touch:** Cold stone, rough wood, oppressive humidity
- **Taste:** Ash in the air, sour wine, stale bread

**What Makes This World Unique:**

1. **Inescapable Prison:** Not just physical but psychological entrapment
2. **Living Villain:** Strahd is omnipresent through agents and appears personally
3. **Cycles and Repetition:** Tatyana reborn; heroes enter and fail; nothing truly changes
4. **Hope as Weapon:** The few glimmers of hope (wine, sunlight, faith) are crucial resources
5. **Genre Mastery:** Pure gothic horror executed perfectly in D&D framework

### History and Backstory

**Ancient History:**

**The Valley Before:**
- Once ruled by peaceful Fey and native humans
- Defended by the Order of the Silver Dragon (dragonborn paladins)
- Prosperous and beautiful under natural sunlight

**Strahd's Conquest (Approx. 400 Years Ago):**
- Strahd von Zarovich, a warrior-prince, conquered the valley
- Drove out the Fey and destroyed the Silver Dragon order
- Named the land "Barovia" after his father, King Barov
- Ruled as mortal lord for decades

**The Tragedy of Tatyana:**

- Strahd fell in love with Tatyana, a young Barovian woman
- She loved Strahd's younger brother, Sergei
- On the night before Sergei and Tatyana's wedding, Strahd made a pact with dark entities
- Strahd killed Sergei and attempted to claim Tatyana
- Tatyana threw herself from Castle Ravenloft rather than submit
- Strahd became a vampire; the Dark Powers trapped the entire valley in mist

**The Curse Established:**

- Dark Powers created Barovia as a prison for Strahd
- The domain became a demiplane in the Shadowfell
- Strahd cannot leave; cannot truly die; cannot have what he wants
- Tatyana reincarnates repeatedly; always escapes him through death

**Centuries of Stagnation:**

- Strahd has ruled for 400+ years
- Periodic attempts by heroes to defeat him (all failed)
- Population slowly declining; hope fading
- Some families serve Strahd (Wachters); others resist (Martikovs)

**Major Historical Events (Last 100 Years):**

**The Dusk Elf Massacre:**
- Rahadin led slaughter of dusk elf women
- Punishment for one dusk elf's attempt on Strahd's life
- Kasimir's sister killed; race doomed to extinction

**The Wizard of Wines Founding:**
- Martikov wereravens established winery
- Three magical gems create extraordinary wine
- Wine becomes Barovia's primary source of hope

**The Abbot's Arrival:**
- Deva sent to redeem Barovia
- Slowly went insane from impossible task
- Now creates flesh golem "bride" for Strahd

**Van Richten's Vendetta:**
- Famous vampire hunter lost son to Vistani and vampires
- Cursed by Vistani mother
- Arrived in Barovia to kill Strahd
- Been preparing for years in disguise

**Recent Events (Last Year):**

- Ireena Kolyana (Tatyana reborn) comes of age
- Strahd begins courting her; attacks their home
- Burgomaster Kolyan Indirovich dies from stress
- Strahd invites new heroes to Barovia (the player)
- Druids corrupt Wizard of Wines gems
- Vallaki's festivals grow more desperate
- Stage is set for final confrontation

**Goals:**
- Serve Strahd (some clans)

- Survive through storytelling and fortune-telling

**Relationships:**
- Allied with Strahd (complicated; some genuinely loyal, others coerced)
- Distrusted by Barovians
- Van Richten hunts them (killed his son)

**Player Interaction:** Can provide aid or betray; Tarokka reading crucial

**Leadership:** Davian Martikov (winery owner)

**Goals:**

**Methods:** Shapechanging into ravens for surveillance; subtle aid

**Relationships:**
- Allied with player (if discovered)
- Opposed to Strahd and druids

**The Order of the Silver Dragon (Destroyed)**

**Leadership:** All dead; spirits linger

**Current State:**
- Ruins at Argynvostholt

**Goals:**
- Overthrow Baron Vallakovich

**Goals:**

## Dialogue Framework

### Dialogue Style

**Overall Style:** Period-Appropriate with Modern Accessibility

**Characteristics:**
- **Formal but not Archaic:** Characters speak with some formality but remain comprehensible
- **Class-Conscious:** Nobles speak differently than peasants
- **Trauma-Informed:** Barovians speak with defeated, hopeless tones
- **Personality-Driven:** Each major NPC has distinct voice

**Formality Levels:**

**Nobility (Strahd, Fiona Wachter):**
- Formal address, complex sentences
- "You honor me with your presence."
- Cultured vocabulary

**Common Folk (Ismark, Innkeepers):**
- Simpler but respectful
- "Thank the gods you've come."
- Practical, direct

**Vistani:**
- Theatrical, mystical
- "The cards speak of your destiny, outsider."
- Rich imagery

**Humor Level:** Dark and Rare

- Gallows humor from survivors
- Strahd's sardonic wit
- Brief moments of levity in camps or taverns
- Mostly serious and melancholic tone

**Profanity/Mature Language:**

- Minimal explicit profanity (D&D appropriate)
- Mature themes (horror, death, obsession) handled seriously
- Violence described but not gratuitously
- Romantic/sexual content implied not explicit (Strahd's obsession)

**Example Dialogue Styles:**

**Strahd (Cultured Menace):**
> "Welcome, traveler. Barovia has need of heroes... though I confess, few survive long enough to prove heroic. Do come in. The dinner grows cold, and I have such *plans* for you."

**Ireena (Frightened but Brave):**
> "He comes every night. I hear him scratching at the window, calling me by... another name. I won't go with him. I won't. But how long can I resist?"

**Ismark (Desperate and Ashamed):**
> "They call me 'the Lesser' because I can't protect my own sister. Father's dead. The town's given up. And you... you're our last hope. Please, help us."

**Madam Eva (Cryptic Oracle):**
> "The cards show a winding path, stranger. Three treasures lost. One great ally. And death... ah yes, death dances throughout your future. But whose death? That remains to be seen."

### Branching Dialogue

**System Design:**

Since this is an LLM-powered text-based RPG, dialogue doesn't use traditional branching trees but rather:

**Dynamic Response System:**
- Player types freeform dialogue or actions
- LLM interprets intent and generates NPC response
- NPC personality and current relationship status influence response
- Major story flags affect available conversation topics

**Conversation Tracking:**
- NPCs remember previous conversations (stored in NPC.md files)
- Relationship values (friendly, neutral, hostile) affect tone
- Key information learned unlocks new dialogue options
- Lies and deceptions can be tracked and called out later

**Branch Points:**

**Major Choice-Driven Branches:**

1. **Ireena's Fate:**
   - Protect and deliver to safety → Various safety locations
   - Allow Strahd to claim her → Tragedy ending
   - Sacrifice to pool → Redemption ending

2. **Vallaki Politics:**
   - Support Baron → Maintain status quo (temporary)
   - Support Wachters → Devil-worshiper rule
   - Stay neutral → Chaos ensues

3. **Dark Gifts at Amber Temple:**
   - Accept gifts → Mechanical power + corruption
   - Refuse gifts → Purity maintained
   - Destroy gifts → Prevent future corruption

4. **Ally Recruitment:**
- Van Richten: Recruit or leave alone
   - Ezmerelda: Work together or separately
   - Martikovs: Earn trust or remain ignorant
   - Each affects final battle support

**Convergence Points:**

Despite branching, major plot points converge:
- Must eventually confront Strahd
- Three artifacts must be found (locations vary)
- Castle Ravenloft is final destination

**Unique Dialogue Volume:**

**Estimated Word Count:**
- **NPC Personality Profiles:** 50+ characters × 300 words = 15,000 words
- **Location Descriptions:** 30+ locations × 200 words = 6,000 words
- **Key Dialogue Scenes:** 20+ scenes × 500 words = 10,000 words
- **Random Encounters:** 20+ encounters × 100 words = 2,000 words
- **Quest Dialogue:** 30+ quests × 400 words = 12,000 words

**Total Base Content:** ~45,000 words of structured dialogue/description

**LLM-Generated Variance:**
- LLM creates infinite variation based on player input
- Core personality and story facts remain consistent
- Each playthrough has unique conversations while hitting same narrative beats

### Timeline

**Ancient History (Before Strahd)**

- **??? Years Ago:** Fey and native humans inhabit the valley
- **~450 Years Ago:** Order of the Silver Dragon established; Argynvost guards the valley
- **~420 Years Ago:** King Barov rules; Strahd born as eldest son

**Strahd's Mortal Life**

- **~400 Years Ago:** Strahd leads military campaigns; conquers surrounding lands
- **~401 Years Ago:** Strahd conquers the valley; names it "Barovia" after father
- **~401 Years Ago:** Strahd destroys Order of the Silver Dragon; takes Argynvost's skull as trophy
- **~400 Years Ago:** Strahd rules as mortal lord; begins to age; fears death

**The Curse Begins**

- **Day of the Curse (400 Years Ago):**
  - Strahd falls in love with Tatyana
  - Tatyana loves Sergei (Strahd's brother)
  - Wedding planned between Sergei and Tatyana
  - Strahd makes pact with dark entities for immortality
  - Strahd murders Sergei on wedding eve
  - Tatyana flees; throws herself from castle spire
  - Strahd transforms into vampire
  - Dark Powers trap entire valley in mists; create Barovia as demiplane prison
  - Castle becomes sentient; loyal to Strahd

**Centuries of Entrapment (400 Years of History)**

- **~380 Years Ago:** First Tatyana reincarnation; dies fleeing Strahd
- **~350 Years Ago:** Second reincarnation; claimed and turned by Strahd (Ludmilla Vilisevic - first bride)
- **~300 Years Ago:** Berez village destroyed by Strahd's wrath when Tatyana reincarnation (Marina) drowns herself
- **~250 Years Ago:** Anastrasya becomes second bride
- **~200 Years Ago:** Abbey of Saint Markovia established; deva sent to redeem Barovia
- **~150 Years Ago:** Volenta becomes third bride
- **~100 Years Ago:** Rahadin leads massacre of dusk elf women; race nearly extinct

**Recent History (Last 50 Years)**

- **~30 Years Ago:** The Mad Mage (Mordenkainen) enters Barovia; battles Strahd; memory erased; wanders insane
- **~20 Years Ago:** The Abbot goes mad from failed attempts to redeem Barovia; begins creating flesh golem bride
- **~15 Years Ago:** Van Richten's son captured by Vistani; sold to vampire; killed
- **~10 Years Ago:** Van Richten cursed by Vistani mother; becomes legendary vampire hunter
- **~5 Years Ago:** Martikovs establish Wizard of Wines winery; three magic gems create wine
- **~3 Years Ago:** Van Richten enters Barovia in disguise (Rictavio the carnival master)
- **~2 Years Ago:** Ezmerelda d'Avenir enters Barovia seeking Van Richten
- **~1 Year Ago:** Ireena Kolyana comes of age; Strahd notices her (Tatyana reborn)

- **3 Months Ago:** First attack on Kolyan home; Ireena bitten twice
- **2 Months Ago:** Druids begin corrupting Wizard of Wines winery
